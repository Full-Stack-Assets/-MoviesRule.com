The use of practical stunts in filmmaking has been a topic of discussion in recent years. With the advancement of CGI technology, many have wondered if practical stunts are still necessary. However, practical stunts bring a level of realism to films that CGI cannot match. They also provide a safer working environment for actors and stunt performers. 
   <Callout type="takeaway"> Practical stunts offer a unique blend of realism and safety that CGI cannot replicate.</Callout>
   ## What happened
   The film industry has seen a significant shift towards CGI in recent years. Many films now rely heavily on CGI for action sequences and special effects. However, some filmmakers still prefer to use practical stunts for certain scenes. This approach has been used in various films, resulting in more realistic and engaging action sequences.
   The use of practical stunts also allows for more creative freedom. Filmmakers can experiment with different techniques and approaches, resulting in unique and innovative action sequences. Additionally, practical stunts provide an opportunity for actors to perform their own stunts, which can enhance their performance and add to the overall authenticity of the film.
   The safety of actors and stunt performers is also a major concern. Practical stunts can be designed to minimize the risk of injury, providing a safer working environment for everyone involved.
   ## Why it matters
   The use of practical stunts has a significant impact on the overall quality of a film. It can enhance the action sequences, provide a safer working environment, and offer more creative freedom for filmmakers. The use of practical stunts also allows for a more authentic and engaging viewing experience for audiences.

   ## How to think about it
   When considering the use of practical stunts in filmmaking, it's essential to weigh the benefits against the potential drawbacks. Filmmakers should consider the type of film they are making, the budget, and the resources available. They should also prioritize the safety of actors and stunt performers.
   <Callout type="warning"> While practical stunts can be beneficial, they can also be hazardous if not designed and executed properly.</Callout>
