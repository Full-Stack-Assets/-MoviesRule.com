Film noir is a term used to describe a style of movie that originated in the 1940s and 1950s. These films are known for their dark and gritty tone, often featuring crime stories and moral ambiguity. The influence of film noir can still be seen in many modern movies and TV shows. Film noir has had a lasting impact on the film industry. 
   <Callout type="takeaway"> Film noir's influence can be seen in many modern movies and TV shows.</Callout>
   ## What happened
   Film noir emerged as a distinct style of movie in the 1940s and 1950s. These films were often based on crime novels and featured tough-talking detectives and femme fatales. The style was characterized by its use of shadows, lighting, and camera angles to create a dark and moody atmosphere.
   The classic film noir period lasted from the early 1940s to the late 1950s. During this time, many classic films were produced, including Double Indemnity and The Big Sleep.
   The style has continued to evolve over the years, with many modern films and TV shows paying homage to the classic film noir style.
   ## Why it matters
   Film noir's influence can be seen in many areas of popular culture, from music to literature. The style has also had a lasting impact on the film industry, with many modern directors citing film noir as an influence.

   ## How to think about it
   To appreciate film noir, it's essential to consider the historical context in which these films were made. Many film noir classics were produced during a time of great social change and upheaval, and the films often reflect this.
