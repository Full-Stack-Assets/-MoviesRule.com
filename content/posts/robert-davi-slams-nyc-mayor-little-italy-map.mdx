Robert Davi, the actor known for his role in the 'Die Hard' franchise, has publicly criticized New York City Mayor Zohran Mamdani after a city map highlighting immigrant enclaves omitted Little Italy. The map, which features 30 immigrant communities across the five boroughs, was criticized by Italian-American groups for excluding historically significant neighborhoods.

    <Callout type="takeaway">The controversy highlights the importance of preserving cultural heritage and recognizing the contributions of immigrant communities to New York City.</Callout>

    ## What happened
    The 'New York City Immigrant Enclaves' map was released by the city, but it omitted Little Italy, as well as historically Jewish and Irish neighborhoods. Following criticism, the city announced plans to update the map to include Little Italy.

    Robert Davi, born in Astoria, Queens, expressed his outrage in a video posted on social media, calling Mayor Mamdani a 'jerk' for snubbing Little Italy. Davi emphasized his connection to his Italian heritage, stating that his grandparents came from Sicily and Naples and taught him to speak English.

    ## Why it matters
    The omission of Little Italy from the map sparked concerns about the erasure of cultural heritage and the contributions of Italian-American communities to New York City. The controversy highlights the need for greater inclusivity and recognition of the diverse immigrant communities that make up the city.
