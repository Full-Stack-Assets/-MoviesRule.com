Fans of high-octane Korean thrillers have a concrete date to mark on their calendars as Disney+ officially schedules the return of one of its biggest international hits. The slick assassin drama *A Shop for Killers* will premiere its second season on July 22, 2026, continuing the story of a young woman thrust into the deadly world of underground arms dealing. This release highlights the streaming platform's ongoing commitment to expanding its Korean-language portfolio to capture global audiences.

<Callout type="takeaway">The return of A Shop for Killers on July 22, 2026, solidifies Disney+’s strategy of leveraging high-profile Korean action franchises to sustain international subscriber growth.</Callout>

## What happened

Disney+ announced that the second season of *A Shop for Killers* will debut with a two-episode premiere on July 22, 2026. The remaining episodes of the eight-part season will roll out in pairs every Wednesday, leading up to the season finale on August 12, 2026. The series will stream on Disney+ internationally and on Hulu for viewers in the United States.

The upcoming season picks up with Jeong Ji-an, played by Kim Hye-jun, installed as the new head of Murthehelp, the secret online storefront that supplies weapons to assassins. She is joined by her uncle, Jeong Jin-man, played by Lee Dong-wook, who faked his death in the first season to draw out his adversaries. Together, the duo will face off against Babylon, the mercenary organization that hunted Ji-an throughout the debut season.

To counter the protagonists, Babylon is importing international talent, introducing new characters played by Japanese actors Hyunri as the team leader Q and Masaki Okada as the mercenary J. Director Lee Kwon returns to helm the season, co-writing the new episodes alongside writer Ji Ho-jin.

## Why it matters

The renewal and rapid production of *A Shop for Killers* Season 2 underscore the critical role Korean dramas play in Disney+'s global streaming strategy. The first season was the platform's most-watched Asia-Pacific original of 2024 until it was surpassed by *Light Shop* in December of that year. By securing returning talent and adding prominent Japanese actors, Disney is aiming to maximize cross-border appeal across Asian markets.

This release is part of a broader push by Disney+ to establish a reliable pipeline of returning K-drama hits. Alongside this series, the streamer is preparing a second season of the 1970s crime drama *Made in Korea* and has a sophomore season of the superhero hit *Moving* in production. This concentrated investment demonstrates that the platform is moving away from one-off acquisitions in favor of building long-term, multi-season franchises.

## How to think about it

For viewers and industry analysts alike, the return of this series represents a maturing phase for streaming platforms producing international content. Rather than relying solely on the novelty of new intellectual properties, platforms are now focusing on the longevity of established intellectual properties. This shift mirrors traditional television structures, where successful shows are expected to build multi-year arcs rather than concluding after a single limited run.

Audiences should prepare for a faster-paced narrative that shifts from the defensive survival tactics of the first season to an active, offensive conflict. The inclusion of new international mercenaries suggests that the world-building will expand beyond local boundaries, raising the stakes for the central storefront.
