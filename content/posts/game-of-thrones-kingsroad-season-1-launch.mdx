Netmarble announced that the free-to-play RPG Game of Thrones: Kingsroad is adding its first major content update, Season 1, on Wednesday, July 15, 2026. The new season, titled The North Bannerman Qualifiers: Frost and Steel, introduces a tournament‑style competition where players represent one of three Great Houses. This surprise launch arrives while the franchise continues to expand across games, TV, and film, giving fans fresh ways to engage with the world of Westeros.

<Callout type="takeaway">Season 1 brings a house‑based competitive tournament that reshapes the Kingsroad experience.</Callout>

## What happened
Game of Thrones: Kingsroad will release Season 1, "The North Bannerman Qualifiers: Frost and Steel," on July 15, 2026 for iOS, Android, and PC via Steam. The update adds a tournament of champions, described as the North’s Olympics, where players pledge allegiance to House Umber, House Hornwood, or House Manderly and compete as that house’s champion.

Individual and house rankings are calculated weekly, with rewards distributed each Wednesday. Scores reset weekly, while each house’s cumulative score builds over a five‑week period, culminating in a final winner announcement.

## Why it matters
The season expands the Game of Thrones universe beyond television, offering an interactive competitive layer that can deepen fan investment. By tying performance to house loyalty, the update encourages community rivalry and sustained engagement, which can translate into longer player lifespans and potential revenue streams for Netmarble. However, the focus on a single tournament mode may limit broader content appeal.
