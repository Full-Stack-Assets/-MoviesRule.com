The aspect ratio of a film can greatly impact the viewing experience. Recently, there has been a trend towards wider aspect ratios in movies. This shift is important because it affects how stories are told and consumed. The use of different aspect ratios can enhance or detract from the overall cinematic experience. Aspect ratios have been a key aspect of filmmaking for decades, with different ratios being used to achieve specific effects. 
   <Callout type="takeaway"> The aspect ratio of a film can significantly influence the viewer's emotional response and engagement with the story.</Callout>
   ## What happened
   The film industry has seen a shift towards wider aspect ratios in recent years. This change is driven by advancements in technology and the desire to create a more immersive experience for viewers. Wider aspect ratios can provide a more cinematic feel, drawing the viewer into the world of the film. The use of different aspect ratios can also be used to convey different emotions and moods. 
   The choice of aspect ratio can depend on the type of film being made, with different genres often utilizing specific ratios. For example, action films often use wider aspect ratios to create a sense of tension and excitement. 
   The trend towards wider aspect ratios is not limited to theatrical releases, as streaming services have also begun to adopt this approach.
   ## Why it matters
   The aspect ratio of a film can have a significant impact on the viewer's experience. A wider aspect ratio can create a more immersive experience, while a narrower ratio can make the film feel more intimate. The use of different aspect ratios can also be used to convey different themes and ideas. 
   The shift towards wider aspect ratios is also driven by the desire to create a more engaging experience for viewers. With the rise of streaming services, filmmakers are looking for ways to differentiate their content and provide a unique experience for viewers.

   ## How to think about it
   When considering the aspect ratio of a film, it is essential to think about the overall goals and themes of the project. The choice of aspect ratio should be driven by the desire to create a specific experience for the viewer. Filmmakers should consider the type of film being made, the target audience, and the overall aesthetic they want to achieve.
