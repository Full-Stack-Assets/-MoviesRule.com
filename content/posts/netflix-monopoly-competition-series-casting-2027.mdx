Netflix has opened casting for a new competition series built around the classic board game Monopoly. The unscripted show will feature 12 contestants battling for a $2 million prize on a life‑sized Monopoly Town Square. Production is being handled by Hasbro Entertainment and Studio Lambert, the team behind Squid Game: The Challenge. The series is slated for a 2027 premiere, expanding Netflix’s slate of game‑based reality programming. Its premise blends familiar board‑game mechanics with high‑stakes elimination, promising a fresh twist for reality fans.

<Callout type="takeaway">Netflix’s Monopoly series aims to turn a beloved board game into a high‑stakes reality competition.</Callout>

## What happened
Netflix announced that casting is now open for a Monopoly competition series. The series will follow twelve contestants as they navigate a fully realized Monopoly Town Square, making deals and alliances to avoid bankruptcy.

The show promises a $2 million grand prize and will eliminate players one by one until a single winner remains. Executive producers include Gabriel Marano and Zachary Edwin from Hasbro Entertainment, along with Nia Yemoh, Tim Harcourt, Jack Burgess, Stephen Lambert and Kim Murphy from Studio Lambert.

The project joins other Hasbro‑based projects in development at Netflix, such as a live‑action Dungeons & Dragons series and an animated Magic: The Gathering series.

## Why it matters
By adapting a universally known game, Netflix taps into built‑in brand recognition, reducing marketing friction and attracting both board‑game fans and reality‑TV viewers. The $2 million prize raises the stakes, positioning the series alongside other high‑budget competition shows and signaling Netflix’s commitment to unscripted content. Successful execution could open a pipeline for further game‑based adaptations, reshaping the reality‑competition landscape.
