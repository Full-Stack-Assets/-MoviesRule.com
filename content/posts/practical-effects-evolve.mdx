The use of practical effects in science fiction films has undergone significant transformations. From the early days of cinema to the current era, filmmakers have continually pushed the boundaries of what is possible. The evolution of practical effects has played a crucial role in shaping the visual identity of science fiction films. As technology advances, the line between practical and visual effects becomes increasingly blurred. The integration of practical effects with computer-generated imagery has enabled the creation of more realistic and immersive cinematic experiences. 
   <Callout type="takeaway"> The evolution of practical effects in science fiction films has transformed the genre's visual landscape.</Callout>
   ## What happened
   The early days of science fiction filmmaking relied heavily on practical effects, such as miniatures, prosthetics, and in-camera tricks. As special effects technology improved, filmmakers began to incorporate more complex and sophisticated techniques into their work. The advent of computer-generated imagery (CGI) in the 1990s revolutionized the field, allowing for the creation of highly detailed and realistic digital environments and characters.
   The use of practical effects in science fiction films has continued to evolve, with many modern filmmakers opting for a hybrid approach that combines practical and visual effects. This approach enables the creation of more nuanced and textured cinematic experiences, as practical effects can add a level of tangibility and realism that is often lacking in purely digital environments.
   The evolution of practical effects has also been driven by advances in materials science and engineering, which have enabled the creation of more sophisticated and realistic props, costumes, and sets.
   ## Why it matters
   The evolution of practical effects in science fiction films has significant implications for the genre as a whole. By enabling the creation of more realistic and immersive cinematic experiences, practical effects have played a crucial role in shaping the visual identity of science fiction films. The use of practical effects also allows filmmakers to create a sense of tangibility and realism, which is essential for engaging audiences and creating a sense of emotional investment in the story.
