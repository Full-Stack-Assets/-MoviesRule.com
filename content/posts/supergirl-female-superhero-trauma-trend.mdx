The Supergirl film, based on the comic book series, features a villain named Krem, who is portrayed as a human trafficker. This change from the original story has been criticized for adding unnecessary themes of exploitation and sexual violence. The film's plot revolves around Supergirl's fight against Krem, who has poisoned her pet, Krypto, and is kidnapping young girls to be "brides" for the all-male Brigand race.

The comic book series, Supergirl: Woman of Tomorrow, features Krem as the leader of the Brigands, intergalactic raiders responsible for genocide and other atrocities. The film's adaptation of Krem's character has been seen as a departure from the original story, with some critics arguing that it alters the message of the movie.

Other female-led superhero movies, such as Black Widow, have also dealt with similar themes of exploitation and sexual violence. The handling of trauma in these movies is a concern, as it can be mishandled and perpetuate negative attitudes towards women.
