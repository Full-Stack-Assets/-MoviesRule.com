Las Vegas enters the game looking to build on their 13-5 record, while the struggling 6-12 Sky hope to secure a consecutive home victory. With key players like Chennedy Carter and DiJonai Carrington carrying crucial injury designations, these roster limitations could fundamentally shift the competitive balance of the game. Fans tuning in will see how these depleted rotations adapt under pressure.

## What happened

The Las Vegas Aces travel to Chicago following a Thursday night home victory against the Dallas Wings, where A'ja Wilson dominated with 32 points and nine rebounds, supported by Jackie Young's 20 points. For Sunday's game, the Aces have listed guard Chennedy Carter as questionable due to an illness, raising the possibility of her return after a five-game absence. However, Las Vegas will remain without Janiah Barker due to a right leg injury, and Dana Evans is still sidelined with a left leg injury.

The Chicago Sky enter the matchup after a dominant Friday night home win against the Portland Fire, led by Kamilla Cardoso's 30 points and Sydney Taylor's 29 points. Despite that momentum, Chicago faces severe roster constraints. The team has officially ruled out guard DiJonai Carrington as she continues her recovery from left foot surgery, and forward Rickea Jackson is out for the remainder of the season with a torn ACL.

## Why it matters

This matchup highlights the stark contrast between a championship-contending Aces squad trying to maintain momentum and a rebuilding Sky team fighting to stay competitive. For the Aces, getting Carter back could provide a massive boost to their backcourt depth as they prepare for the season's harder stretches. Conversely, the Sky must find long-term offensive solutions without Carrington and Jackson, placing immense pressure on young stars like Cardoso and Taylor to carry the scoring load.
