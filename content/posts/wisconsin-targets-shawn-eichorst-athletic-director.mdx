The University of Wisconsin is reportedly targeting Texas deputy athletic director Shawn Eichorst to fill its vacant athletic director position. According to reports, the Wisconsin native and former deputy AD under Barry Alvarez is poised for a homecoming to Madison. This potential hire comes at a critical juncture as the Badgers look to revitalize their athletic department and address growing fan apathy surrounding their football program.

<Callout type="takeaway">
Wisconsin is leaning on familiar leadership by targeting Shawn Eichorst, a veteran administrator with deep ties to the university, to navigate a challenging transition period for its athletic programs.
</Callout>

## What happened

According to a report by ESPN's Pete Thamel, the Wisconsin Badgers are targeting Shawn Eichorst for their athletic director vacancy. A university spokesman did not immediately respond to the report. Eichorst, a Lone Rock native and UW-Whitewater alumnus, previously served as a deputy athletic director at Wisconsin under Barry Alvarez before taking athletic director roles at Miami from 2011 to 2012 and Nebraska from 2012 to 2017. He has spent the last several years at the University of Texas, serving as deputy athletic director and chief operating officer since 2018.

The search for a permanent athletic director was aided by CSA Search and Consulting, with a hiring committee chaired by social work professor Lauren Bishop. The committee also featured notable sports executives, including Indianapolis Colts general manager Chris Ballard, New Jersey Devils executive Meghan Duggan, and Dallas Mavericks executive Michael Finley. Marcus Sedberry, a deputy AD and football general manager, had been serving as the interim athletic director but did not publicly indicate whether he was seeking the permanent role.

## Why it matters

Eichorst would inherit an athletic department facing immediate pressure, particularly within its marquee football program. Under head coach Luke Fickell, the football team is coming off back-to-back losing seasons, a rare slump that has fueled growing apathy among the fanbase. Bringing in an experienced administrator who understands the unique culture and history of Wisconsin athletics could help stabilize the department. However, Eichorst's past track record at Nebraska—which included the controversial firing of football coach Bo Pelini and the subsequent hiring of Mike Riley—will likely be closely scrutinized by fans and analysts as he takes the reins in Madison.

Evaluating this potential hire requires balancing Eichorst's deep institutional knowledge against the mixed outcomes of his previous athletic director tenures. His familiarity with the Wisconsin system means he will not face the steep learning curve of an outsider, allowing him to address pressing issues like football program stability right away.
