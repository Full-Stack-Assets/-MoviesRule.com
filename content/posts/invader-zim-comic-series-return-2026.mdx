The cult classic Nickelodeon animated franchise Invader Zim is officially set to return in 2026 with a brand-new comic book series. Published by BOOM! Studios, this upcoming release marks the property's first major comic run since its previous publication era ended five years ago. While fans are eager to dive back into the dark comedy of the Irken empire, the announcement has been met with a mix of excitement and behind-the-scenes tension regarding the involvement of the franchise's original creator. This revival highlights the enduring legacy of the early 2000s animation era and the complexities of managing beloved intellectual properties.

## What happened

BOOM! Studios, known for handling major licensed properties like Power Rangers and Buffy the Vampire Slayer, announced that a new Invader Zim comic series will debut later in 2026. The project will feature prominent industry veterans, including writer Jim Zub, who has worked on Marvel, DC, and Adventure Time projects, and artist Dax Gordine, known for his work with Marvel. This marks a transition for the franchise, which was previously published by Oni Press from 2015 to 2021.

However, the announcement quickly revealed a communication gap between the publisher and series creator Jhonen Vasquez. On July 3rd, Vasquez shared on Instagram that BOOM! Studios had not contacted him about the new series, meaning he learned of the revival at the same time as the general public. While Vasquez did not take an aggressive stance, he expressed disappointment at the lack of direct outreach.

In response, writer Jim Zub published a blog post explaining that he accepted the writing role under the impression that Vasquez had already approved the project. Zub expressed sympathy for Vasquez's situation and noted that BOOM! Studios has since reached out to the creator to rectify the oversight. The exact nature of Vasquez's potential involvement or the resolution remains unconfirmed.

## Why it matters

This situation underscores the delicate balance publishers must strike when reviving beloved cult properties. For a franchise like Invader Zim, which relies heavily on its creator's highly specific, eccentric artistic voice, proceeding without the creator's direct blessing risks alienating a fiercely loyal fanbase. The success of previous revivals, such as the Oni Press comic run and the Netflix film Invader Zim: Enter the Florpus, was largely attributed to Vasquez's direct oversight and creative input.

For BOOM! Studios, resolving this public relations hiccup is crucial to maintaining fan goodwill. The publisher has a strong track record of delivering critically acclaimed adaptations, but starting a high-profile launch with a public misunderstanding could dampen initial sales. For the broader comic book industry, it serves as a reminder of the ethical and practical importance of creator relations, even when a publisher legally holds the licensing rights to a property.

## How to think about it

When evaluating the potential of this new series, readers should separate the business-side missteps from the actual creative talent involved. Jim Zub and Dax Gordine are highly respected professionals with deep appreciation for the source material, and Zub's immediate, transparent response to Vasquez's post shows a commitment to respecting the franchise's roots. It is best to watch how BOOM! Studios integrates Vasquez's feedback moving forward before writing off the project entirely.
