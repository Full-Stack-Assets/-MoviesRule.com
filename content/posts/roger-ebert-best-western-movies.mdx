The Western genre has experienced dramatic shifts in Hollywood popularity over the last century, nearly vanishing from mainstream production during the 1960s. This transitional era coincided with the rise of legendary film critic Roger Ebert, who evaluated thousands of movies, including over one hundred Westerns, throughout his career. By examining the select traditional Westerns that Ebert awarded a perfect four-star rating, fans can gain a deeper appreciation for the structural and thematic heights of the genre. Understanding these critical selections helps modern viewers identify which historical films truly transcend their era's tropes.

<Callout type="takeaway">
Roger Ebert's highest-rated Westerns stand out because they elevate traditional genre conventions through exceptional character depth and progressive thematic elements.
</Callout>

## What happened

To curate a definitive list of Ebert's favorite Westerns, the selection was narrowed down to films that received his coveted four-star rating. Furthermore, the focus was tightened to traditional Westerns set in the late 19th century, excluding modern-day settings with only minor exceptions. Out of more than one hundred Westerns reviewed by Ebert, only ten traditional masterpieces were highlighted to represent his ultimate standards for the genre.

Among these top selections are landmark films like John Ford's 1939 classic Stagecoach and Nicholas Ray's Johnny Guitar. Stagecoach is celebrated for its symphonic pacing and ensemble cast, which launched John Wayne into major stardom despite director John Ford having to fight for his casting. Meanwhile, Johnny Guitar stands out for its unique gender politics, placing Joan Crawford and Mercedes McCambridge in dominant, traditionally male roles that challenge the standard conventions of the period.

## Why it matters

Ebert's critical curation emphasizes that the best Westerns are not merely about gunfights and lawlessness, but are complex studies of human behavior and societal shifts. By championing films like Johnny Guitar, Ebert highlighted how the genre could subvert traditional gender roles and explore progressive themes long before these discussions became mainstream in Hollywood. His focus on character-driven narratives over simple action beats provides a roadmap for understanding how classic cinema remains relevant to modern audiences. This perspective helps preserve the legacy of filmmakers who pushed the boundaries of a highly formulaic genre.

## How to think about it

When exploring these classic Westerns, viewers should look beyond the dusty landscapes and standard tropes of the frontier. The key is to analyze how these films use confined spaces—such as a moving stagecoach or an isolated saloon—to examine class differences, social tension, and human morality under pressure. By focusing on the psychological depth of the characters rather than the inevitability of the final shootout, audiences can appreciate why these specific films earned Ebert's highest praise. This analytical approach transforms a simple viewing experience into a study of cinematic craftsmanship and storytelling endurance.

<Callout type="warning">
Viewers should be prepared for outdated cultural depictions, particularly regarding Native Americans, which reflect the era in which these classic films were produced.
</Callout>
