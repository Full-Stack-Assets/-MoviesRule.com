The three-act structure is a fundamental concept in screenwriting. It has been used in countless films and television shows. This structure typically includes a setup, a confrontation, and a resolution. The three-act structure is important because it provides a clear framework for storytelling. 
   <Callout type="takeaway"> The three-act structure is a key element of successful screenwriting.</Callout>
   ## What happened
   The three-act structure has been used in many successful films. It was first developed by Syd Field, a well-known screenwriting instructor. The structure is based on the idea that a story should have a clear beginning, middle, and end. 
   The first act sets up the story and introduces the main characters. The second act presents obstacles and conflicts that the characters must overcome. The third act resolves the conflicts and provides a sense of closure. 
   The three-act structure is flexible and can be adapted to fit different types of stories.
   ## Why it matters
   The three-act structure is important because it provides a clear framework for storytelling. It helps writers to create a compelling narrative with a clear beginning, middle, and end. The structure also helps to build tension and create suspense.

   ## How to think about it
   To use the three-act structure effectively, writers should think about how to create a clear and compelling narrative. This involves developing well-rounded characters, creating tension and conflict, and providing a satisfying resolution. 
   <Callout type="warning"> Be careful not to make the structure too formulaic or predictable.</Callout>
