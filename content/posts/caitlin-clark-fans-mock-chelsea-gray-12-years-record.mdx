When Chelsea Gray logged a 15‑point, 11‑assist double‑double against the Phoenix Mercury, she finally tied Caitlin Clark’s WNBA record for the fourth‑most double‑doubles with points and assists. The feat came in Gray’s 12th season, while Clark reached the same mark in just three years. Fans of the Indiana Fever seized the moment, flooding social media with jokes about the “12‑year vet” finally catching up. The exchange underscores the growing rivalry between the veteran point guard and the league’s breakout star. It also raises questions about how the league values longevity versus rapid production.

## What happened
Chelsea Gray, a 12‑year veteran of the Las Vegas Aces, recorded 15 points and 11 assists in a 101‑50 victory over the Phoenix Mercury, tying Caitlin Clark for the fourth‑highest total of double‑doubles with points and assists (22 each). Gray achieved this in her 391st game, while Clark reached the same total in just 71 appearances over three seasons.

Caitlin Clark had previously tied Gray’s total on June 16 with a 21‑point, 14‑assist performance for the Indiana Fever. She then added a points‑and‑rebounds double‑double on June 19, bringing her overall double‑double count to 22. Both players now sit behind Courtney Vandersloot (62), Alyssa Thomas (39) and Sue Bird (28) on the all‑time list.

The two guards are slated to meet again when the Fever visit Las Vegas, with Clark listed as probable despite a back injury. The matchup offers a direct comparison of a rising star versus a seasoned playmaker.

## Why it matters

This dialogue can influence viewership, as fans tune in to see the “record race” play out on the court. Moreover, the conversation spotlights the rarity of point‑guard double‑doubles, a metric that traditionally favors big‑men, thereby reshaping how the league celebrates guard play.
