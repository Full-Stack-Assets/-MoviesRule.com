## What happened

Friday's schedule features critical matchups across three groups. In Group I, Norway and France meet at 3 p.m. ET at Boston Stadium to decide the group winner. Both teams have already secured advancement with six points each, and the match highlights a scoring duel between Erling Haaland and Kylian Mbappe, who have each scored four goals. France holds the tiebreaker advantage and will win the group with a win or a draw. Meanwhile, Senegal and Iraq play concurrently in Toronto, where only a win keeps their slim hopes of advancing as a top third-place team alive.

At 8 p.m. ET, Group H concludes with Spain facing Uruguay in Guadalajara, while Cape Verde plays Saudi Arabia in Houston. Spain leads the group with four points and carries a 33-game unbeaten streak in competitive play. Uruguay, sitting on two points after disappointing draws, likely must defeat Spain to advance. Cape Verde, the tournament's surprise package with a population of just 525,000, also sits on two points and can secure a historic knockout spot with a victory over Saudi Arabia.

The late-night slot at 11 p.m. ET features Belgium taking on New Zealand at BC Place in Vancouver to wrap up Group G. Both teams likely require a victory in this final match to guarantee progression to the round of 32. All six matches will be broadcast on FOX or FS1 and will stream live on FOX One.
