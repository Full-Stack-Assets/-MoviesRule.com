The Coalition of Asian Pacifics in Entertainment (CAPE) is celebrating its 35th anniversary with a gala in Downtown Los Angeles on October 3. Manny Jacinto and Randall Park will be honored at the event, which will be hosted by Asif Ali and feature a performance by indie band Chanpan. CAPE has been dedicated to creating opportunities for Asian and Pacific Islander talent in Hollywood since its launch in 1991. This gala marks a milestone for the organization, which typically hosts an anniversary event every five years, but had to adjust its schedule due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The event is presented by Lexus, with additional support from Sony Pictures Entertainment and Suntory Global Spirits.

## What happened
CAPE is celebrating its 35th anniversary with a gala event in Downtown Los Angeles. The gala is presented by Lexus and supported by Sony Pictures Entertainment and Suntory Global Spirits.

Randall Park, known for his roles in Fresh Off the Boat, Aquaman, and WandaVision, was previously honored by CAPE with a new writers award in 2011. He is also an alumnus of CAPE's programs, which have helped launch the careers of other notable talent like filmmakers Alice Wu, Beth de Araújo, and Liz Sargent.
