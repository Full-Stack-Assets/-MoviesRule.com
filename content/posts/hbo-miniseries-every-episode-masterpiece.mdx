    ## What happened
    HBO's legacy in miniseries production extends back to the 1970s, with notable examples including Angels in America and Sharp Objects. Angels in America, based on Tony Kushner's Pulitzer Prize-winning play, explores the AIDS crisis in 1985 New York through six interconnected lives. The series revolutionized the art of the miniseries with its blend of bleak reality and metaphysical spectacle.

    Sharp Objects, a psychological thriller, follows Camille Preaker as she returns to her hometown to investigate murders and confront her troubled mother. The series is a character study that explores themes of trauma, family, and resilience.
