Spain captured its second FIFA World Cup title on Sunday, edging Argentina 1‑0 after extra time at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey. Ferran Torres delivered the decisive goal in the 112th minute, sealing a narrow victory for the European side. The win comes 16 years after Spain’s first triumph in 2010 and caps a tournament co‑hosted by the United States, Mexico and Canada. For Argentine fans, the loss marked Lionel Messi’s third World Cup final appearance, a record for any player. The match’s broadcast on Fox and Telemundo drew millions of viewers, underscoring the global appetite for the sport.

## What happened
Spain defeated Argentina 1‑0 after extra time, with Ferran Torres scoring the only goal of the match. The final was played at MetLife Stadium (referred to as New York New Jersey Stadium for the tournament) in East Rutherford, New Jersey, and was televised on Fox and Telemundo.

The tournament was notable for being the first World Cup jointly hosted by three nations— the United States, Mexico, and Canada. It also marked Messi’s third appearance in a World Cup final, a feat no other player has achieved.

## Why it matters
Spain’s victory re‑establishes the nation as a powerhouse in modern football, echoing their 2010 triumph and influencing future tactical approaches across Europe. For Argentina, the loss ends a long‑awaited quest for a title despite Messi’s historic personal milestone, signaling a potential period of rebuilding. The co‑hosting model and the global broadcast reach demonstrate the tournament’s expanding commercial and cultural impact, setting a precedent for the 2030 World Cup’s multi‑continent format.
