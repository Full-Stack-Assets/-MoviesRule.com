Barbara Ling, the Oscar-winning production designer for Quentin Tarantino's 'Once Upon a Time in Hollywood' and the Michael Jackson biopic 'Michael', has died at the age of 73. She passed away on Thursday in Santa Barbara after being diagnosed with cancer. Ling's career spanned over four decades, including work on more than 200 theater, opera, and musical productions, as well as numerous films. Her work on 'Once Upon a Time in Hollywood' earned her an Oscar for Best Production Design, along with set decorator Nancy Haigh, as well as an Art Directors Guild Award and a Critics' Choice Award. Ling's legacy profoundly influenced the art of production design and the visual language of modern filmmaking.
