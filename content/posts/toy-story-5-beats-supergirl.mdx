The box office saw a significant upset this weekend, with Toy Story 5 maintaining its top spot and Supergirl opening to a disappointing $40M domestic. This comes as a surprise, given the hype surrounding the film. The low opening can be attributed to the finicky nature of fanboys and fangirls when it comes to big screen superhero properties.

<Callout type="takeaway">Toy Story 5 stays on top, while Supergirl's opening is lower than expected.</Callout>

## What happened
Toy Story 5 is still ruling the box office, with a second weekend around $70M+ depending on the turnout from families. However, Supergirl filed a lower-than-expected $40M domestic opening. The global start looks to hover around $75M, with $11M so far abroad from 78 markets and 40,000 screens. CinemaScore was a B-, which is lower than other DC films.

The definite recommend on Screen Engine/Rentrak's PostTrak is 52%, which is low for a planned summer tentpole of this magnitude. Men gave Supergirl a very low definite recommend at 45%, while women were slightly better at 62%. Tracking firms saw this coming, with Supergirl being one of the wobbly event films this summer.

## Why it matters
The low opening of Supergirl has significant implications for the film industry. It highlights the challenges of expanding demographics beyond older men and the importance of having a sticky marketing campaign. James Gunn and Peter Safran are tasked with delivering filmmaker-driven fare that isn't cookie cutter, and this film's performance may impact their strategy.
