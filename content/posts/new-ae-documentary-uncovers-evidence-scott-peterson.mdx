A new two‑part documentary titled “Scott Peterson: The New Evidence” will air on A&E July 16‑17, promising footage and handwritten notes never seen by the public. The series features defense lawyer Chris Pixley and retired detective Ninette Toosbuy revisiting the 2002 murder of Laci Peterson and the disposal of her body. Supporters say the material could raise enough doubt to merit a new trial for the man serving life for the crime. The release arrives as the Los Angeles Innocence Project has already filed motions citing the same evidence. If the claims hold, the case could become one of the most high‑profile reversals in recent memory.

## What happened
The A&E network announced a two‑part documentary, “Scott Peterson: The New Evidence,” scheduled for July 16 and 17. The program presents previously unseen surveillance footage, alleged handwritten notes attributed to Laci, and commentary from legal analysts.
In 2024 the Los Angeles Innocence Project took over Peterson’s case, filing court documents that cite the same newly discovered items as potential proof of innocence. The organization argues that the evidence raises unanswered questions about who actually abducted and killed Laci and their unborn son.
Peterson was convicted in 2004 after a jury found him guilty of murdering his pregnant wife and their unborn child, largely based on his alleged disposal of the body from his fishing boat on Christmas Eve 2002.
## Why it matters
If the documentary’s evidence withstands scrutiny, it could trigger a formal review of a high‑profile murder conviction, influencing how post‑conviction claims are evaluated nationwide. A reversal would affect victims’ families, the criminal‑justice system’s credibility, and public perception of true‑crime media’s role in legal advocacy.
