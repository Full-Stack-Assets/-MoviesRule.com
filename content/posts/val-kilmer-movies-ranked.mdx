## What happened
Val Kilmer's career spanned multiple genres and roles. In Top Gun, he played the rival Tom "Iceman" Kazansky, bringing a sense of discipline and arrogance to the character. In The Doors, he portrayed Jim Morrison, capturing the singer's charisma and self-destructive tendencies. 
Kilmer's performances were often marked by his commitment to the role, including doing his own singing in The Doors. His ability to convey complexity and depth on screen made his characters memorable and impactful. 
The success of these movies can be attributed to Kilmer's talent and the directors' vision. Oliver Stone's direction in The Doors, for example, allowed Kilmer to explore the darker aspects of Jim Morrison's personality.
## Why it matters
Val Kilmer's movies continue to influence contemporary cinema. His performances in Top Gun and The Doors have become iconic, with many regarding them as career-defining roles. The impact of these movies can be seen in the way they have shaped the action and biographical drama genres. 
Kilmer's ability to bring depth and nuance to his characters has inspired other actors to follow in his footsteps. His legacy serves as a reminder of the importance of taking risks and pushing boundaries in filmmaking. 
The relevance of Kilmer's movies extends beyond the screen, with their themes and characters continuing to resonate with audiences today. As a cultural touchstone, his filmography provides a window into the past, while also offering insights into the human condition.
