The Despicable Me franchise has taken an unexpected detour into film history with its latest spinoff, Minions & Monsters. Set in 1920s Hollywood, the animated feature follows a distinct trio of Minions—James, Henry, and Ed—as they transition from silent film stars to accidental monster summoners. Directed by returning franchise veteran Pierre Coffin, the movie has already set a new high-water mark for the series, earning a 91% Certified Fresh rating on Rotten Tomatoes. This critical success highlights how the franchise is successfully evolving by pairing its signature slapstick humor with sophisticated historical homages.

## What happened

In Minions & Monsters, director Pierre Coffin returns to the franchise to guide a new group of Minions through the turbulent transition from silent films to talkies. When their careers are threatened by the advent of sound, the trio uses an ancient spellbook to summon real monsters for a film project, inadvertently putting the world in danger. The voice cast features an ensemble including Trey Parker, Allison Janney, Christoph Waltz, Jesse Eisenberg, Jeff Bridges, and Zoey Deutch.

The film is packed with specific nods to classic cinema that the cast and crew recently highlighted. Allison Janney pointed out a direct nod to Casablanca and its iconic song "As Time Goes By," while Jeff Bridges praised a physical comedy sequence mirroring Buster Keaton's famous falling-house stunt. Jesse Eisenberg noted a subtle reference to the early cinema myth of audiences fleeing a projected train, and Coffin himself highlighted a multi-layered tribute to Orson Welles's Citizen Kane.

Beyond these historical nods, the movie features a shocking cameo by Star Wars creator George Lucas, marking his first acting role in 19 years. Lucas appears as himself inside a museum's glass box, a gag that surprised cast member Bobby Moynihan during production.

## Why it matters

This shift to a historical period piece demonstrates how Illumination is expanding the narrative boundaries of its most lucrative intellectual property. By focusing on a separate tribe of Minions in the 1920s rather than relying on the established Gru dynamic, the filmmakers prove the brand's versatility. The record-breaking 91% Rotten Tomatoes score suggests that critics appreciate this artistic pivot, which respects adult cinephiles with genuine historical Easter eggs while keeping the physical comedy accessible to children.

## How to think about it

View Minions & Monsters as a dual-layered viewing experience rather than a standard children's movie. The film operates on two distinct frequencies: one of pure, kinetic slapstick for younger viewers, and another of historical appreciation for film buffs. When watching, pay close attention to the background details and set designs, as director Pierre Coffin has indicated that many of the best cinematic tributes are throwaway visual gags hidden in plain sight.
