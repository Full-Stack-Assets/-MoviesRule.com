The Hays Code was a set of moral guidelines that governed the production of American films. It was established in the 1930s and had a significant impact on the film industry. The code prohibited the depiction of explicit content, profanity, and other forms of mature themes. This led to a period of creative censorship in Hollywood, where filmmakers had to find ways to convey complex ideas and themes without violating the code. The Hays Code played a crucial role in shaping the types of films that were produced during this time.

   ## What happened
   The Hays Code was introduced in 1930 and was enforced by the Motion Picture Association of America. It was created in response to growing concerns about the content of films and their potential impact on society. The code was designed to promote decency and morality in films, and it prohibited the depiction of explicit content, profanity, and other forms of mature themes. 
   The code had a significant impact on the film industry, leading to a period of creative censorship in Hollywood. Filmmakers had to find ways to convey complex ideas and themes without violating the code, which often resulted in subtle and suggestive storytelling. 
   The Hays Code also led to the establishment of the Production Code Administration, which was responsible for enforcing the code and ensuring that films complied with its guidelines.
