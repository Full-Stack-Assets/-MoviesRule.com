Washington, D.C. has passed legislation that makes it the nation's capital with the most stringent anti-scalping marketplace in the U.S. for concert tickets. The District of Columbia Council unanimously passed the RESALE Act, capping ticket resale prices at 10 percent above the ticket's original face value. This move aims to curb markups on secondary ticketing sites.

<Callout type="takeaway">The RESALE Act introduces a 10% cap on ticket resale prices and bans speculative tickets.</Callout>

## What happened
The RESALE Act, passed by the District of Columbia Council, introduces several key measures. It caps ticket resale prices for concerts at 10 percent above the ticket's original face value. The act also bans speculative tickets, where ticket brokers list tickets for sale that they don't actually possess yet. Additionally, those who sell more than 50 concert tickets per year on resale markets will be required to register with D.C.

The legislation is significant as Washington, D.C. is a major touring stop with venues like the Capital One Arena and Nationals Park. The city's policies could serve as a framework for similarly sized markets.

## Why it matters
The new policy aims to protect consumers from high markups on secondary ticketing platforms. Fans have expressed frustration with the concert market due to issues like high fees, long queues, and dynamic pricing. By capping resale prices and banning speculative tickets, the RESALE Act puts fans first and strengthens the District's independent stages.

## How to think about it
When buying concert tickets, fans should be aware of the new regulations in Washington, D.C. The 10% cap on resale prices and ban on speculative tickets aim to create a fairer market. Fans should also consider registering with the city if they sell more than 50 tickets per year.

<Callout type="warning">Fans should be cautious of ticket sellers who may try to circumvent the new regulations. Always verify the legitimacy of ticket sellers and be aware of the terms and conditions of ticket purchases.</Callout>
