Season 2 of Disney+’s X‑Men ’97 has confirmed a high‑profile addition to its voice cast. Erika Ishii, a veteran of video‑game narration and most recently Mortal Kombat 1, will voice the iconic villain Lady Deathstrike. The character appears in Episode 5, “Weapon X, Lies, and DVDs,” where she confronts Wolverine at a new Weapon X facility. This move blends two fan‑bases and signals the series’ commitment to deep comic‑book lore. Existing fans and newcomers alike will notice the crossover impact.

## What happened

The fifth episode of Season 2 introduces Lady Deathstrike, voiced by Erika Ishii, as Wolverine’s longtime adversary during a Weapon X infiltration. The announcement was confirmed in Collider’s recent report, noting that Ishii joins other notable voice talent such as Darin De Paul (Sabretooth) and Crispin Freeman (Maverick). Episode 5 also pits the mutant duo against the Brood, advancing the Weapon X storyline that began in Season 1.

Ishii’s résumé includes prominent roles in Dragon Age: The Veilguard, Apex Legends, and the fighting‑game franchise Mortal Kombat 1. Her transition to Marvel animation highlights the growing trend of video‑game actors crossing into superhero media, bringing a built‑in fan following.

## Why it matters

Bringing a Mortal Kombat star into X‑Men ’97 expands the series’ appeal beyond traditional comic readers, attracting gamers who recognize Ishii’s voice work. It also reinforces Disney+’s strategy of leveraging recognizable talent to differentiate its animated revivals from the original 1990s series. For the narrative, Lady Deathstrike’s presence deepens Wolverine’s personal arc, offering fresh conflict while honoring long‑standing comic continuity.
