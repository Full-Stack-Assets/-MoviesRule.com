The 2026 World Cup Round of 16 kicked off with high-stakes drama as France and Morocco secured their places in the quarterfinals. France narrowly defeated a resilient Paraguay side 1-0 in a physical encounter, while Morocco shut out tournament co-host Canada with a decisive second-half performance. These results set up a highly anticipated quarterfinal clash between the two sides in Boston on July 9. For soccer fans worldwide, this double-header showcases the intense competitive balance and tactical drama of the knockout stages.

## What happened

In the opening match of the Round of 16, co-host Canada faced Morocco in a lively first-half battle but failed to convert their chances. Morocco adjusted their tactics after halftime, adopting a more aggressive approach that paid off handsomely. Azzedine Ounahi scored twice, and Soufiane Rahimi added a stoppage-time goal to seal a 3-0 victory, making Morocco the first African nation to reach the quarterfinals in consecutive World Cups.

Later in the day, tournament favorite France faced a defensive Paraguay squad in a highly physical match. Kylian Mbappé broke the deadlock in the 70th minute by converting a penalty kick, reclaiming the lead in the Golden Boot race. Despite intense pressure from Paraguay in the final minutes, including a close save by French goalkeeper Mike Maignan and ten minutes of stoppage time, France held on for a 1-0 victory.

## Why it matters

The outcomes of these matches establish significant milestones for both programs. Morocco's progression cements their status as a modern powerhouse in international soccer, proving that their historic 2022 run was not a fluke. For France, surviving a highly physical, controversial match demonstrates their resilience, though the disciplinary fallout and physical toll could impact their depth. This sets up a tactical chess match in Boston, where France's star-studded offense will meet Morocco's disciplined, aggressive defensive structure.
