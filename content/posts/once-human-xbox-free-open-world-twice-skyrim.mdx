Xbox has added the free‑to‑play RPG Once Human to its console lineup, expanding the service’s catalog with a massive open‑world experience. The game’s map spans 256 square kilometers—roughly twice the size of The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim’s world. Once Human blends looter‑shooter mechanics, survival crafting, and both PvE and PvP combat across a post‑apocalyptic landscape. The launch on Xbox Series X|S and PC arrives with cross‑play, cross‑progression, and optional keyboard‑mouse support. Its sheer scale promises more than 100 hours of exploration for players who prefer a single‑player‑friendly environment.

<Callout type="takeaway">Once Human delivers a free, cross‑play open world that dwarfs Skyrim’s map, setting a new benchmark for console RPGs.</Callout>

## What happened
Once Human, developed by Starry Studio, debuted on Windows in 2024 and later on mobile before arriving on Xbox Series X/S, Xbox PC, and PlayStation 5 on 25 August 2026. The title is free to download and play, and it offers a 256 km² (16 km × 16 km) map that the publisher describes as twice the size of Skyrim’s terrain.

The game mixes looter‑shooter and survival elements, allowing players to gather resources, build bases, and defend against both AI monsters and human raiders. Cross‑progression lets you carry your character and inventory across PC and console, while keyboard‑mouse support on console mirrors the PC experience.

A noted limitation is that the console build lags behind the PC version in terms of content updates. Starry Studio has indicated that future patches will bring the console iteration up to parity with its PC counterpart.

## Why it matters
By delivering a free, massive‑scale RPG, Xbox strengthens its position in the competitive free‑to‑play market and offers a compelling alternative to subscription‑based services. The map’s size alone suggests a longevity that rivals many premium titles, potentially keeping players engaged for months without additional purchases. However, the update lag could fragment the player base, prompting some to favor the more current PC version.

## How to think about it
Start by completing the introductory missions to grasp the survival mechanics before venturing far from your initial base. Prioritize gathering crafting materials and upgrading your shelter, as these directly affect your ability to survive PvP raids. Leverage cross‑play to join larger groups for high‑risk zones, but be mindful of the mixed PvE/PvP environment. If you play on a console, enable keyboard‑mouse mode for finer control during combat and building.

<Callout type="warning">The console version does not yet include the latest content patches released on PC, so expect missing events and gear until parity is achieved.</Callout>
