## What happened
Project Runway returns to Freeform for its 22nd season, featuring 22 designers—double the usual roster—with six contestants eliminated in the premiere episode. The judging panel keeps core members Heidi Klum and Nina Garcia, adds Law Roach, and brings Tyra Banks back as a recurring judge, raising the stakes for the designers.

Peacock launches The Five-Star Weekend, a romantic‑comedy series set around a weekend getaway that critics describe as fluffy and addictive, making it a strong candidate for a weekend binge. Hulu’s catalog adds new episodes of Letterkenny, the Canadian sitcom known for its rapid‑fire jokes and rural setting, giving fans fresh material to enjoy.

## Why it matters
Project Runway’s expanded format reflects a broader trend in reality TV toward larger, more diverse contestant pools, which can boost viewer engagement and social‑media conversation. The Five-Star Weekend’s light‑hearted tone offers a counterpoint to heavier dramas, catering to audiences seeking escapism after a long day. Letterkenny’s return on Hulu demonstrates how niche comedies can thrive on streaming platforms, attracting both loyal fans and newcomers, and reinforcing the value of a mixed‑genre slate for subscriber retention.
