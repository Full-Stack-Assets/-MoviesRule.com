Marathon’s longtime game director Joseph Ziegler announced his departure from Bungie on July 17, 2026. In a brief X post, Ziegler confirmed today is his final day overseeing the Sony‑backed extraction shooter. He named assistant director Del Chafe III and creative director Julia Nardin as the duo steering Marathon forward. The shift comes amid Bungie’s recent layoffs and a $765 million impairment loss tied to underperformance. Fans and investors are watching to see how leadership changes will affect Marathon’s upcoming Season 3 launch.

<Callout type="takeaway">Marathon will continue under new leadership as its director departs.</Callout>

## What happened
Joseph Ziegler, who joined Bungie in December 2022 and led Marathon since its March 2026 launch, posted on X that July 17, 2026 is his last day. He praised assistant director Del Chafe III and creative director Julia Nardin, stating they are ready to guide the game into its next chapter.

The announcement arrives after Bungie reported a $765 million impairment loss linked to Marathon’s underperformance and announced significant layoffs affecting many Marathon team members. The studio has hinted at big ideas for future story content, but the departure adds uncertainty as the game moves toward Season 3 in September.

## Why it matters

New leads may prioritize different features, influencing player experience and retention during the critical Season 3 rollout. Additionally, the change occurs alongside financial strain, potentially affecting resource allocation and future updates.
