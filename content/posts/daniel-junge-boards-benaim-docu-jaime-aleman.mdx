Oscar and Emmy winner Daniel Junge has signed on as executive producer for Abner Benaim’s next documentary. The film will profile Jaime Alemán, the 72‑year‑old Panamanian lawyer‑diplomat who has visited every UN‑recognized country, both poles and even space. Production has already spanned locations from the Azores to Bangkok, and the team is now gearing up for a trek to the remote island of Tristan da Cunha. The partnership reunites Junge with Benaim after their award‑winning “Tropical Paradise,” raising expectations for a compelling blend of adventure and introspection.

<Callout type="takeaway">Junge’s involvement signals a high‑profile push for a documentary that blends extreme travel with a deeper personal quest.</Callout>

## What happened
Daniel Junge, known for Oscar‑winning work on “Saving Face,” is executive‑producing the untitled documentary directed by Panama’s Abner Benaim. The project follows Jaime Alemán, a lawyer, diplomat and businessman who has visited all 193 UN‑member states, reached both the North and South Poles, and flew on Blue Origin’s New Shepard NS‑32 suborbital mission last year. Benaim told Variety that Alemán’s outward journey serves as a vehicle to explore his inner psyche, vulnerabilities and the motivations that keep him moving.

The crew has already captured footage in the Azores, Brazil, Toronto and Panama, with upcoming shoots slated for Bangkok, Bhutan and Pakistan. Their next major expedition will take Alemán to Tristan da Cunha, one of the world’s most isolated uninhabited islands, reachable only after a five‑day sea voyage.

## Why it matters
The documentary merges two compelling narratives: a record‑breaking travel saga and an intimate portrait of a man confronting his own purpose. By attaching a filmmaker of Junge’s stature, the film gains greater visibility on the global festival circuit, potentially opening doors for wider distribution on streaming platforms. For audiences, the story offers a rare look at how extreme external exploration can mirror an internal search for meaning, resonating in an era where travel content dominates but deeper human stories are scarce.
