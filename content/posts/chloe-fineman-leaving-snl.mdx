Chloe Fineman, a standout cast member on Saturday Night Live, has announced she's leaving the show after seven seasons. Fineman, who joined SNL during the 2019/2020 season, confirmed her departure on Instagram, stating it's the right time for her to move on. She expressed her gratitude to Lorne Michaels and the entire SNL team, calling her experience on the show the greatest privilege of her life.

<Callout type="takeaway">Chloe Fineman is leaving SNL to pursue other projects, including a role in Netflix's forthcoming Harlan Coben adaptation 'Myron Bolitar'.</Callout>

## What happened
Fineman spent seven seasons on SNL, becoming a beloved member of the cast. Her decision to leave is her own, unlike some other cast members who were fired. Fineman has been acting more in recent years, appearing in shows like 'Big Mouth' and 'Laid,' as well as movies such as 'Summer of 69,' 'Freakier Friday,' and Francis Ford Coppola's 'Megalopolis.'

## Why it matters
Fineman's departure is another big loss for SNL, following Bowen Yang's exit after seven seasons. Lorne Michaels and NBC will need to find fresh talent to fill the void for Season 52. Fineman's decision highlights the challenges of balancing SNL commitments with other acting projects.
