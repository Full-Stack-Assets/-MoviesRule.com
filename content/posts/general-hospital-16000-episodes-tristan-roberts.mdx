General Hospital aired its landmark 16,000th episode on ABC this week, a milestone that cements its status as the longest‑running scripted drama in U.S. television. The episode brought back fan‑favorite Jason Morgan and reunited Anna and Valentin, while also introducing a new surgeon, Dr. Tristan Roberts. Roberts is a direct homage to veteran actor Tristan Rogers, who portrayed Robert Scorpio for more than four decades before his death in August 2025. The celebration was highlighted by a cake‑cutting ceremony on the Prospect Studios lot, and the episode will be available later on Hulu. For longtime viewers and newcomers alike, the moment signals both continuity and fresh storytelling.

## What happened
The 16,000th episode opened with Jason Morgan (Steve Burton) returning to Port Charles, New York, and featured an emotional reunion between Anna (Finola Hughes) and Valentin (James Patrick). New surgeon Dr. Tristan Roberts, played by Dean Geyer, was introduced in the ER, his name chosen as a tribute to Tristan Rogers, the longtime Robert Scorpio who passed away in August 2025. A cake‑cutting ceremony on the Prospect Studios lot marked the milestone, and the episode will stream on Hulu after its weekday ABC broadcast.
In its 64th year, General Hospital remains the longest‑running scripted American drama and the longest‑running soap opera still in production. The series premiered in 1963, has earned 18 Daytime Emmy wins, and once drew 30 million viewers for a 1981 episode. Notable guest stars have included Elizabeth Taylor and John Oliver, underscoring the show’s cultural reach.
