Ryan Hurst’s bicep tear on the Disney+ “God Of War” set has forced the series into an unexpected production pause. The lead actor, who plays Kratos, suffered a full‑muscle tear during a stunt, requiring immediate surgery. With filming suspended, the show’s debut could slip to as late as 2027, reshaping Disney’s upcoming slate.

## What happened
Sources confirmed that Hurst tore his bicep while performing a stunt on the Vancouver set. The injury was diagnosed as a full‑muscle tear, necessitating surgical repair.

The schedule had originally targeted a mid‑August return for Hurst, but the recovery period extends well beyond that date. Consequently, filming has been suspended and executives are now eyeing a possible 2027 release.

Other cast members, including Ólafur Darri Ólafsson as Thor and Mandy Patinkin as Odin, remain on standby as the studio revises its shooting calendar.
