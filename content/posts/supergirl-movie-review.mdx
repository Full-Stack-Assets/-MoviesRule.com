The new Supergirl movie has been released, and the reviews are in. Unfortunately, the film has been panned by critics for its terrible script and lackluster action sequences. The movie follows the story of Kara Zor-El, played by Milly Alcock, as she navigates a dystopian world filled with danger and uncertainty. Despite its promising premise, the film falls flat due to its poorly written characters and unengaging plot.

<Callout type="takeaway">The Supergirl movie is a disappointing addition to the DC Studios franchise, with a weak script and unimpressive action sequences.</Callout>

## What happened
The movie opens with Krypto the superdog peeing on a newspaper headline about Superman saving a small town. From there, the film introduces us to Kara Zor-El, who is an interplanetary drunk in a Blondie T-shirt, bopping from one arid dystopia to the next. The villain, Krem of the Yellow Hills, is an overly derivative "Mad Max" reject, played by Matthias Schoenaerts with a shaved head and scraggly ponytail. The movie's plot is centered around Kara's quest to retrieve an antidote for Krypto, who has been poisoned by Krem.

The movie's action sequences are muddled and unimpressive, with too much repetition and not enough tension. The characters are also poorly written, with Kara being a one-note protagonist who lacks depth and complexity. The supporting cast is equally underwhelming, with Ruthye Marye Knoll being a particularly poorly written character.

## Why it matters
The Supergirl movie's failure is a significant blow to the DC Studios franchise, which has been struggling to find its footing in recent years.
