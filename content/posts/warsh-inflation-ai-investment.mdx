Fed Chairman Kevin Warsh has promised to defeat inflation and 'get monetary policy right.' In remarks delivered to Congress, Warsh vowed to tackle inflation, citing the economy's solid shape and benefits from artificial intelligence investment. The Federal Reserve Chairman emphasized the central bank's commitment to restoring price stability.

<Callout type="takeaway">Warsh promises inflation will be a 'thing of the past' due to the Fed's commitment to monetary policy and AI investment boom.</Callout>

## What happened
Warsh, who took over as Fed Chairman two months prior, reiterated his tough stance on inflation in remarks to the House Financial Services Committee and Senate Banking Committee. He emphasized the Fed's number one objective is to get monetary policy right, which will help bring down inflation. Warsh noted that persistently high inflation levels have been an undue burden on American households and businesses.

The Fed Chairman highlighted that underlying inflation over longer time horizons is determined largely by monetary policy. He expressed optimism about the economy's prospects, citing benefits from business investment, particularly in artificial intelligence.

## Why it matters
The stakes are high for Warsh and the Fed, as inflation has exceeded the 2% mandate since 2021. The Chairman's commitment to defeating inflation and getting monetary policy right will have significant implications for American households and businesses. If successful, Warsh's approach could lead to a decrease in the cost of living and stabilize the economy.
