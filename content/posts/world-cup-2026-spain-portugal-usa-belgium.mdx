## What happened

In a tightly contested round-of-16 match, Spain defeated Portugal 1-0 courtesy of a dramatic goal in the 90th minute. The defeat eliminated Portugal and likely concluded the World Cup career of legendary forward Cristiano Ronaldo, who had previously indicated this tournament would be his last. This high-stakes matchup followed a wave of major tournament exits, including Mexico's 3-2 loss to England at the Estadio Azteca and Norway's elimination of powerhouse Brazil.

In Seattle, the United States faced Belgium with forward Folarin Balogun in the starting lineup despite a red card received on July 1 against Bosnia and Herzegovina. Normally, a red card carries an automatic one-match suspension, but FIFA rescinded the penalty after the United States government appealed directly to the organization. Senator Ted Cruz publicly thanked President Donald Trump for personally contacting FIFA leadership to resolve the suspension, sparking widespread debate over political interference in international sports.

With Balogun cleared to play, the American squad, managed by Mauricio Pochettino, sought its first quarterfinal appearance since 2002. The match began with immediate tension as U.S. goalkeeper Matt Freese was forced to make a crucial save just 30 seconds into the game to deny an early Belgian goal.
