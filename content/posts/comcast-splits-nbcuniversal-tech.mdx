Comcast is separating its media business, including NBCUniversal and Sky, from its technology business. This move is expected to create two public companies. The separation is planned to be completed in about a year. Comcast's chief, Brian L. Roberts, will continue to be involved in the leadership of both companies. The media business will be run by Mike Cavanagh, while Michael Angelakis will become the CEO of Comcast after the separation.

   ## What happened
   Comcast announced that it will separate its media business from its technology business. The media business will include NBCUniversal, Sky, and other assets. The technology business will focus on delivering customer experiences through its converged network and global technology platforms.
   The separation is expected to be completed in about a year and will result in two public companies. Comcast shareholders will own shares in both companies.
   The move is seen as a way to unlock a more entrepreneurial management approach and open up new opportunities for each business.
   ## Why it matters
   The separation of Comcast's media and technology businesses is significant because it will create two focused industry leaders. The media business will have significant scale and a strong financial profile, while the technology business will have a strong foundation for growth.
   The separation will also allow each business to pursue its own strategic opportunities and make decisions that are in the best interest of its shareholders.
