Amazon's Prime Video has successfully revived a beloved comedy franchise with its new prequel series, Elle. Premiering on July 1, the show transports viewers back to the mid-1990s to explore the origin story of one of cinema's most iconic protagonists. Its rapid ascent to the top of the streaming charts demonstrates that audiences remain deeply invested in this twenty-five-year-old intellectual property. This sudden success highlights a growing industry trend of using prequel television to breathe new life into classic cinematic brands.

<Callout type="takeaway">
The instant streaming success of Elle proves that nostalgic, character-driven prequels can capture massive modern audiences when paired with the right streaming platform.
</Callout>

## What happened

On July 1, Prime Video released the full season of Elle, a prequel series to the Legally Blonde films. Within days, the show became a certified hit, ranking among the platform's Top 10 most popular TV shows in over 50 countries. In the United States, the series quickly claimed the number one spot on the streaming service's charts.

Set in 1995, the series stars Lexi Minetree as a 16-year-old Elle Woods. The narrative follows the young protagonist as she is pulled out of her comfortable Beverly Hills lifestyle following a scandal involving her family. This marks the first major entry in the franchise in over a decade, representing a significant gamble on a dormant intellectual property that has immediately paid off for the studio.

## Why it matters

The success of Elle reinforces a broader Hollywood strategy where studios leverage established intellectual property through television prequels. Following the footsteps of successful 2025 prequels like Alien: Earth and IT: Welcome to Derry, this release demonstrates that the prequel formula works across diverse genres, not just in horror or science fiction. For Prime Video, securing a top-performing comedy-drama franchise helps solidify its position in a highly competitive streaming landscape. It also proves that younger audiences and nostalgic older fans alike are willing to show up for reimagined legacy characters, even when the original lead actor is replaced by a newcomer.

## How to think about it

When analyzing the success of Elle, it is helpful to view it as part of a calculated industry shift toward low-risk, high-reward intellectual property development. Rather than funding expensive, unproven original concepts, streaming platforms are finding that audiences prefer familiar names with fresh faces. For viewers, this means we can expect more legacy film franchises to receive the television prequel treatment in the near future. To evaluate whether these shows are worth your time, look beyond the initial hype and focus on how well the new cast captures the spirit of the original characters while offering a distinct narrative voice.

<Callout type="warning">
Viewers expecting a direct continuation of the original films or a cameo from the original cast may find the 1995 high school setting and new ensemble cast jarring.
</Callout>
